## 🚀 **Project Overview**
This project presents an in-depth analysis of **India’s Consumer Price Index (CPI) Inflation** from **2013 to 2023** using Excel.

◆ The goal is to identify:
- **Key drivers of inflation**
- **Sector-wise contribution**
- **Trend and volatility patterns**

◆ The analysis highlights how **Food, Fuel, and Healthcare** significantly influence overall inflation.

---

## 🎯 **Objectives**
- Analyze **CPI inflation trends over time**
- Identify **dominant sectors driving inflation**
- Study **volatility in food & fuel prices**
- Generate **data-driven economic insights**

---

## 🔹 **Key Insights**

###  **Food & Beverages = Biggest Driver**
- ~45–49% weight in CPI basket  
- Strongest impact on overall inflation  

---

### 🔹 **Inflation Peak (2022)**
- Reached **~7.8%**
- Highest since 2017  
- Driven by **post-COVID recovery & global shocks**

---

### 🔹 **Food Inflation Volatility**
- Ranged between **3.2% – 8.5%**  
- Influenced by **seasonality & supply issues**

---

### 🔹 **Fuel & Light Impact**
- Reached up to **10.2%**
- Strong correlation with **global crude oil prices**

---

### 🔹 **Healthcare Inflation Surge**
- Increased by **~5.6% post-COVID**
- Indicates rising **medical costs**

## 📖 **Key Concepts**

### 🔹 **What is CPI?**
CPI measures the **average change in prices of goods and services over time**.

---

### 🔹 **What does 7.8% inflation mean?**
If a product cost ₹100 last year → it now costs **₹107.80**

---

### 🔹 **What does 49% weight mean?**
◆ Food contributes **~half of CPI calculation**  
◆ Any change in food prices heavily impacts inflation  

---

## 🔹 **Business & Policy Insights**
- Controlling **food inflation is critical**
- Fuel prices impact **multiple sectors indirectly**
- Strong supply chains can reduce **price volatility**
